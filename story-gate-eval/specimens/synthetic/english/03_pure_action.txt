The Roof
He dropped from the fire escape onto the gravel roof, rolled, came up running. Behind him two of them had already cleared the railing. The next roof was a four-foot gap. He took it without slowing.
Bullet — close — the air popped beside his ear. He cut left behind the AC unit, came out on the far side, and threw himself flat against the parapet.
Below: a delivery truck, a kid on a bike, a woman in a red coat. He counted three breaths and went over the side, swinging by his hands until his feet found the awning.
The awning held. He hit the sidewalk hard. By the time they reached the parapet he was three streets away.
