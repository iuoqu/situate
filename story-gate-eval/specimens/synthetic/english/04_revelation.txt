The Drawer
After my aunt died I was sent to clear her flat. I had been told it would take a weekend. She was a tidy woman; there wasn't much.
In the bottom of the writing desk I found a drawer I hadn't known about, set behind a false panel. Inside, a stack of letters tied with string. All addressed to my mother. None opened.
The postmarks ran across forty-one years. The last was from two weeks ago.
I sat down on the floor of the flat and looked at the dust I had stirred up. My mother had told me, my whole life, that she and her sister had not spoken since before I was born.
The letters were unopened. So my mother had been the silent one. My aunt had written every month for forty-one years.
