After the Recital
Marta had played the second movement too fast. She knew it before she finished. In the green room afterwards her teacher said nothing about the tempo. Only: "You looked tired tonight. Are you sleeping."
"I'm sleeping fine."
"Good."
At the reception a man she didn't know came up and said the Schubert had moved him. He spoke with such directness she felt embarrassed for him. He left after one glass of wine.
On the train home Marta thought she might cry, but didn't. She thought about the man. He had not mentioned the second movement. He had not mentioned anything technical at all.
She had been playing for twenty-two years. She realised — sitting on the train, watching the suburbs go past — that her teacher had never once said something moved him.
