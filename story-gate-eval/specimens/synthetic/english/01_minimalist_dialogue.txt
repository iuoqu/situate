The Booth
They sat in the booth by the window. The rain hadn't stopped since morning.
"You should eat something."
"I'm not hungry."
"You said that yesterday."
"Yesterday I wasn't hungry either."
He pushed the menu across. She didn't look.
"It's been three days."
"Has it?"
"You know it has."
She watched the rain. A man crossed the street under a black umbrella.
"I'll have the soup," she said.
"Good."
"Don't say good like that."
"Like what."
"Like I did something."
He didn't answer. The waitress came. He ordered for both.
When the soup arrived she looked at it for a long time before picking up the spoon.
