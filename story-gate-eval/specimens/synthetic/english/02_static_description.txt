The Kitchen at Dawn
The kitchen faces east. At dawn the light enters at a low angle, catching the steam from the kettle and turning it briefly gold. The tile floor is original — small hexagonal pieces, white with black grout. A wooden table sits in the centre, scarred along one edge where the previous owners must have chopped without a board. Two chairs. A bowl of lemons by the window. The refrigerator hums in C-sharp. A clock somewhere ticks but is not visible from the doorway. The air smells faintly of coffee and yesterday's bread.
